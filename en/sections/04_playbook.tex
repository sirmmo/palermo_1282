\chapter{The Playbooks}
\markboth{The Playbooks}{The Playbooks}

\lettrine[lines=3]{E}{ach} character in \textit{Vespri 1282} is defined
by a \textbf{playbook}: a historical archetype fixing their narrative
role, peak actions, and central tension. Each playbook provides:
\textbf{peak actions} (rating 2), \textbf{secondary actions} (rating 1),
a \textbf{Gift} (special ability), a \textbf{Burden} (structural
weakness), and an opening \textbf{Bond} with another PC in the same
faction.

\section{The Conspirators — Playbooks}

\subsection{The Messenger}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Ruggero di Amato}\\
\textit{You carry words that can change the world. Or get you killed.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Manoeuvre, Deceive\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Act, Persuade, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- Multiple Faces:} You have at least three ready false
identities. When you use one, your Deceive roll is always at least
Controlled position, unless they have already seen you in that face.

\textbf{Burden --- The Chain:} Every message you carry binds you to
its sender. If captured, the GM may call for a Desperate Resist roll
to avoid revealing the source. If you fail, the faction loses 1 point.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Conspirator knows your real name.
You are the only one who does.

\filetto

\subsection{The Conspirator}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Gualtiero de Caltagirone}\\
\textit{You know the plan. Or at least, your piece of it.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Plot, Study\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Deceive, Persuade, Command, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- Compartmentalisation:} Once per session, you may
declare that something you are doing was ``part of the plan'' from
the beginning, even if it was not previously stated. This does not
change the dice — it changes the narrative.

\textbf{Burden --- The Weight of Knowing:} You know things the others
do not. When another faction PC fails a Study roll to understand the
situation, you may choose to reveal the information — but suffer
1 Stress for doing so.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Messenger carries your words.
You hope they are careful enough.

\filetto

\subsection{The Spy}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Margherita di Brindisi}\\
\textit{You work inside the Angevin system. You have for too long.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Deceive, Study\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Manoeuvre, Act, Persuade, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- Inside the Palace:} You have physical access to
spaces others cannot reach: the Norman Palace, the justiciars' offices,
the military quarters — as long as you maintain your cover.

\textbf{Burden --- Contamination:} You have lived among the French
too long. When you act against an Angevin official with whom you have
a personal relationship, roll Resist or suffer 1 Stress from hesitation.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Conspirator recruited you three years ago.
You have not yet forgiven yourself for accepting.

\filetto

\subsection{The Merchant}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Bartolomeo Lancia}\\
\textit{Money has no flag. But you do.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Persuade, Plot\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Deceive, Command, Manoeuvre

\textbf{Gift --- Trade Network:} You have contacts in every Sicilian
port and beyond. You may request information, goods, or safe passage
without a roll — but each request has a narrative cost.

\textbf{Burden --- The Open Account:} You funded part of the conspiracy
with your own money. If the uprising fails, you are ruined. Whenever
a plan seems to waver, roll Resist or act selfishly.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Spy provided you with false documents that
saved your life. You have not yet repaid them.

\filetto

\subsection{The Deserter}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Drogo de Metz}\\
\textit{You fought for Charles of Anjou. Then you saw what you were defending.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Fight, Resist\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Act, Manoeuvre, Command, Defy

\textbf{Gift --- Military Discipline:} When you protect another PC from
physical harm, the harm is reduced by 1 box. You may do this even in
Desperate position without a roll.

\textbf{Burden --- The Known Face:} Angevin soldiers in Palermo
recognise you. Whenever you move in patrolled areas, the GM may declare
that someone has spotted you — unless you took specific precautions.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Messenger convinced you to switch sides.
You were not difficult to convince.

\filetto

\subsection{The Noble in Exile}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Tancredi di Chiaramonte}\\
\textit{Descendant of a family Frederick loved. Charles hates.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Command, Persuade\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Plot, Resist, Defy

\textbf{Gift --- The Ancient Name:} Your surname opens doors among
Sicilian barons. When you present yourself openly, barons cannot
ignore you — even if they do not trust you.

\textbf{Burden --- The Target:} You are on the Angevin surveillance
list. Every time you step into the open, the GM may raise the Angevin
alert level by one degree.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Conspirator knows your brother, still in
Barcelona. The news is not always good.

\filetto

\subsection{The Forger}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Niccolò the Cretan}\\
\textit{Documents, seals, identities. Everything that does not exist, you create.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Deceive, Act\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Plot, Manoeuvre, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- The Right Document:} Once per scene, you may declare
you have a document that resolves a bureaucratic or access problem.
No roll required — but it only holds until examined by an expert.

\textbf{Burden --- The Trace:} Every document you produce carries a
stylistic signature. After the third document used in the session,
the GM may declare that someone has noticed the similarity.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Merchant is your main client.
They know too much about you to be just a client.

\filetto

\subsection{The Sea Captain}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Riccardo della Loggia}\\
\textit{Three languages, four flags, one crew. When the right
wind blows, you are at someone's service.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Manoeuvre, Command\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Persuade, Deceive, Resist

\textbf{Background:} Riccardo captains a mid-sized lateen ship
that works three routes: Palermo--Barcelona,
Palermo--Constantinople, Palermo--Tunis. Born in Trapani, he
learned Greek in Negroponte and Catalan in Mallorca. His
\textit{Stella di Mar} has carried cipher letters for da Procida,
Byzantine gold for the Palermo conspirators, and legitimate silk
for the Angevin court that officially pays him. None of his
three patrons knows of the others. Or pretends not to know.

\textbf{Gift --- The Secret Route:} Once per session, you may
declare you have set up a fast and secret route --- by sea, between
two ports --- delivering one specific cargo (letters, money, a
person) without Angevin interception. The arrival happens
off-screen; the consequences arrive on-screen.

\textbf{Burden --- The Watched Port:} Charles's fleet is taking
shape in Palermo. Every time you use the secret route, the GM
advances the ``harbour watch'' clock. When it fills, your ship is
impounded --- and you cannot leave Palermo by sea for the rest
of the session.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Merchant has paid you for three
crossings. They have not yet paid for the fourth. You both know why.

\subsection{The Baron}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Simone di Mazzarino}\\
\textit{You have land, men, and obligations to both. And to your son.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Command, Persuade\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Plot, Defy, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- The Given Word:} When you make an agreement with
another character and declare it explicitly, both gain +1 die on rolls
relating to that agreement while it holds. If one breaks it, the other
gains a bonus die against them.

\textbf{Burden --- The Weight of the Fief:} Your decisions affect
more than you. Whenever you choose a risky action, the GM may ask:
\textit{and your men? And your family?} You must answer before rolling.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Knight is loyal to you. You hope that
loyalty survives the choices you are about to make.

\textbf{Peak actions:} Fight, Defy\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Command, Resist, Act, Manoeuvre

\textbf{Gift --- Training:} When in combat with at least one ally
nearby, you are always in Risky position (not Desperate) unless

\textbf{Burden --- The Old Oath:} You swore to Charles of Anjou (or
a vassal) when you had no choice. That oath still exists. Every time

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Baron protects you.

\textbf{Peak actions:} Command, Plot\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Persuade, Study, Resist, Deceive

\textbf{Gift --- The Underestimation:} Once per scene, you may declare

opponent is in Controlled position, even if circumstances would suggest

\textbf{Burden --- Visibility:} A woman who commands is visible.
Every Manoeuvre or Deceive roll in French-controlled areas starts at

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Notary holds your documents.
Documents that should not exist.

\subsection{The Notary}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Matteo di Termini}\\
\textit{The law is a tool. The question is who wields it.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Study, Plot\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Persuade, Deceive, Command, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- Living Archive:} You know the legal precedents,
treaties, and royal concessions of Norman and Hohenstaufen Sicily.
You may use Study to find a legal basis for almost any action —
which does not make it safe, but makes it justifiable.

\textbf{Burden --- The Uncomfortable Document:} You hold a document
that could shift the balance of power — and endangers you merely by
possession. The GM knows what it says. You have not yet read it to
the end.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Chatelaine trusts you.
You fear that trust is misplaced.

\subsection{The Son}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Goffredo di Mazzarino}\\
\textit{Your father negotiates. You want to act.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Defy, Act\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Fight, Resist, Incite, Manoeuvre

\textbf{Gift --- Impetus:} When you act first in a crisis situation,
without waiting for instructions, gain +1d. If your action succeeds,
add +1 to the faction score.

\textbf{Burden --- Inexperience:} Your starting position in any
situation requiring negotiation or diplomacy is always Risky, never
Controlled. You are still learning.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Knight taught you to fight.
They have not yet taught you when not to.

\filetto

\subsection{The Physician}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Master Filippo Carafa da Salerno}\\
\textit{You treat everyone, even the French. Especially the French — they know things.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Study, Persuade\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Resist, Deceive, Act, Invoke

\textbf{Background:} Trained at the School of Salerno, Filippo Carafa
arrived in Palermo with an impeccable reputation — and certain interests
the School would not approve. Alongside Galenic medicine, he has studied
Arabic treatises that circulate only in restricted circles: herbs,
infusions, preparations that heal where standard remedies fail and bring
sleep where pain does not relent. He found in Yusuf ibn Khalaf al-Balarmì
a precious interlocutor — the man knows things no Salernitan physician
does — and in Maimone di Palermo a reliable supplier of materials
unavailable in official markets. The Baron knows about the School.
He does not know about the rest.

\textbf{Gift --- The Patient's Trust:} When you have treated someone,
they are in your debt. You may request information or a small favour
from anyone you have treated during the session, without a roll —
but only once per person.

\textbf{Burden --- The Hippocratic Oath:} You cannot refuse to treat
someone in need, not even an Angevin soldier. If you do, suffer
2 immediate Stress.

\textbf{Opening Bonds:} The Baron pays you. But you also treat the
poor of the Albergheria, free, at night. \textit{Cross-faction bonds
(optional):} Yusuf ibn Khalaf al-Balarmì (Clergy faction) is your
Arabic medicine consultant — you have exchanged knowledge for years.
Maimone di Palermo (People faction) supplies what cannot be found in
the apothecaries. Both know things about you the Baron must never learn.

\subsection{The Traitor's Wife}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Costanza Ventimiglia}\\

\textbf{Peak actions:} Resist, Deceive\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Plot, Persuade, Study, Manoeuvre

\textbf{Gift --- The Double Life:} In Angevin eyes, you are the

\textbf{Burden --- The Divided House:} Your husband does not know

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Chatelaine is your cousin.

\filetto

\subsection{The Matriarch}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Donna Margherita Lancia}\\
\textit{You have buried two husbands. You have married off six
daughters. You have not left your house in eleven years. And yet
all Palermo comes to you.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Plot, Persuade\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Command, Resist, Invoke

\textbf{Background:} Donna Margherita Lancia buried her husband
(fallen at Benevento in 1266) and her firstborn son (fallen at
Tagliacozzo in 1268). She married six daughters into as many
baronial houses across the island. Her four grandsons are
everywhere. For eleven years she has not left her \textit{palatium}
in the Vucciria. She does not need to: baronial Sicily comes to
her. She receives in her study, two at a time, without witnesses.
She knows more about the Sicilian nobility than is written in the
registers of the Magna Curia. Perhaps because she wrote much of it.

\textbf{Gift --- The Thread of Houses:} When you speak of a
baronial family with another character (PC or NPC), you may declare
a specific bond --- a marriage, a debt, a wrong --- that ties them
to your house. You and the GM agree what it is, but it must be
plausible. The character must take it seriously: it shapes how
they speak with you for the rest of the scene.

\textbf{Burden --- The House Waits:} Your authority is your house.
If you act in ways that publicly threaten it (signing a compromising
compact in your own name, taking sides openly), the GM advances
the ``succession'' clock --- your sons and grandsons are consulting
each other. When it fills, your house formally acts against
your wishes.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Notary keeps the chest of your private
papers. He knows almost everything. The almost is what counts.

\section{The Clergy — Playbooks}

\subsection{The Bishop}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Berardo di Castronovo}\\
\textit{Serve God. Serve Rome. Serve your people. Choose.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Command, Invoke\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Persuade, Study, Plot, Resist

\textbf{Gift --- Episcopal Authority:} Once per session, you may issue
an order that no faithful Catholic (PC or NPC) can ignore without
spiritual consequences. Those who disobey suffer 1 Stress from guilt.

\textbf{Burden --- Rome Watches:} Your movements are reported to the
curia. If you act too openly against Martin IV's interests, you risk
removal or excommunication. The GM holds a Rome clock.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Preaching Friar is your eyes and ears
on the street. You are not sure how much they trust you.

\filetto

\subsection{The Preaching Friar}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Fra' Tommaso di Caltagirone}\\
\textit{The word of God is a weapon. It depends only on who wields it.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Incite, Invoke\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Persuade, Resist, Study, Manoeuvre

\textbf{Gift --- The Crowd:} When you address a group of people
(at least five), your Incite roll always has Standard or Great effect
— never Limited. Crowds listen to you.

\textbf{Burden --- The Fervour:} Once per session, the GM may declare
you pushed too far: the crowd reacted in a way you did not intend.
Suffer 2 Stress and manage the consequences.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Sacristan watches your back.
They have seen things they do not forget.

\filetto

\subsection{The Canon}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Pandolfo di Messina}\\
\textit{You know the cathedral's secrets. Including those that were never confessed.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Study, Plot\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Persuade, Deceive, Resist, Invoke

\textbf{Gift --- The Archive:} Once per session, you may declare you
found information in the cathedral archives relevant to the current
situation. The GM decides what it contains, but it must be narratively
useful.

\textbf{Burden --- The Seal of Confession:} You know things you cannot
reveal. Some concern people in this story. The GM will tell you at
session start one thing you know and cannot say.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Bishop promoted you.
You are not certain why.

\filetto

\subsection{The Abbess}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Sister Adalasia di Troina}\\
\textit{Your convent is a refuge. For whom is up to you.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Command, Resist\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Persuade, Plot, Invoke

\textbf{Gift --- Sanctuary:} Your convent is sacred ground. French
soldiers cannot enter without violating canon law. You may hide
people, documents, or messages here — but everything hidden is
a responsibility.

\textbf{Burden --- My Sisters:} You have dozens of women under your
protection. Before every action that might draw Angevin attention to
the convent, roll Resist or suffer 1 Stress from the weight of
responsibility.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Preaching Friar brings you news from
the world. It is never good enough.

\filetto

\subsection{The Sacristan}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Arnaldo Gennarino}\\
\textit{You control the keys, the bells, the timing. More power than it seems.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Act, Manoeuvre\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Resist, Deceive, Invoke

\textbf{Gift --- The Bells:} You can ring the bells of Santo Spirito.
This is an act the entire city hears. Used at the right moment, it
automatically adds +1 to the faction score. Used at the wrong moment,
it may trigger an immediate Angevin response.

\textbf{Burden --- The Visibility of the Gesture:} You are the only
one who can ring those bells. If something goes wrong that evening,
everyone will know it was you.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Canon taught you to read.
You owe them more than that.

\filetto

\subsection{The Arab Physician}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Yusuf ibn Khalaf al-Balarmì}\\
\textit{Your family was here before the Normans. And after the Angevins.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Study, Persuade\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Act, Resist, Deceive, Invoke

\textbf{Gift --- The Long Memory:} You know Palermo better than anyone
— the alleys, the passages, the wells. Once per scene, you may declare
you know an alternative route, automatically removing any physical
obstacle to movement.

\textbf{Burden --- The Stranger at Home:} You are Arab and Christian —
or Arab and still Muslim in private, decide with your GM. Either way,
you are suspect to everyone. Command rolls with people who do not
know you always start at Risky.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Abbess protected you once.
You do not forget.

\filetto

\subsection{The Pilgrim}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Luca di Bari}\\
\textit{You arrived in Palermo by chance. Or perhaps not.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Manoeuvre, Deceive\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Study, Resist, Persuade, Invoke

\textbf{Gift --- Nobody Knows Me:} You are not on any Angevin
surveillance list. Your Manoeuvre rolls in controlled areas always
start from Controlled position.

\textbf{Burden --- Nobody Knows Me:} You have no resources, contacts,
or safe base. Every time you need logistical support, you must roll
to find it.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Preaching Friar fed you on your first day.
You told them your real name. No one else knows it.

\filetto

\subsection{The Friar Minor}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Fra' Andrea da Caltagirone}\\
\textit{You have nothing. You want nothing. That is why everyone
talks to you.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Persuade, Manoeuvre\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Resist, Incite, Study, Invoke

\textbf{Background:} Fra' Andrea took the Franciscan habit six
years ago, after watching his father starve to death in Augusta.
He walks barefoot in winter. He eats what is given. He has no
fixed dwelling: he sleeps at the door of whoever takes him in.
The people of Albergheria pass him a bowl of water when they see
him approach. The French officials never arrest him --- not from
respect, but because they never know where he is. He hears
confession on the street. He baptises children in the poor
quarters. He has never asked a coin for anything. He knows things
the Bishop does not.

\textbf{Gift --- Poverty as Shield:} You have less than anyone.
You own nothing that can be stolen or seized. You are always
welcome in any poor house in Sicily: you may enter, sleep, eat,
gather information without a roll in the popular quarters.
People speak in front of you because you are already one of them.

\textbf{Burden --- The Vow:} You may not possess money, weapons,
or valuables --- even if they are offered to you for the cause.
If you must handle any of these (to carry, to hand on), suffer
1 Stress per act. The purity of your life is a real burden.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Preaching Friar respects and despises
you in equal measure. The two of you represent two different
answers to the same question. Tonight you may discover which
answer was right.

\subsection{The Innkeeper}
{\cinzel\footnotesize\color{grigionote} Sabella d'Albergheria}\\
\textit{You serve sour wine to everyone --- French, conspirators,
soldiers, preachers. You hear it all. You say nothing. The back
door, you never close.}

\textbf{Peak actions:} Study, Persuade\\
\textbf{Secondary actions:} Deceive, Resist, Manoeuvre, Incite

\textbf{Background:} Sabella's tavern stands in the alley climbing
toward the Cathedral, at the edge of Albergheria. Sour wine from
Monte San Giuliano, stale bread reheated on the embers, two
tables and one long bench. Open from vespers to dawn. When the
French soldiers come, they sit at the table by the door --- Sabella
serves them in silence. When the artisans from Cassaro come for
meetings they do not want on any record, they sit in the back room.
She closes the main door. She does not close the back door. She
knows who came, when, with whom, and how much they drank. She does
not forget.

\textbf{Gift --- The Back Door:} Your tavern is neutral territory.
Once per scene, you may declare that two characters (PCs or NPCs)
who would not speak to each other anywhere else have found themselves
at your back-room table. The conversation happens in narratively
neutral position --- neither threat nor alliance --- until one of
them breaks it.

\textbf{Burden --- Serve Everyone, Ally to No One:} To remain open,
you must serve anyone who pays. If you openly take a side, the GM
(at the other side's request) may shut your tavern down. If it shuts,
you lose your main tool for the rest of the session --- and the
People lose one of their few spaces.

\textbf{Opening Bond:} The Craftsman comes every Thursday evening.
He owes you three months of wine. In return he fixed the back-door
lock for you. You know the lock opens to a specific picklock.
He does not know that you know.
